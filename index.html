Mind, Machine, and Will: A Stress Test

About This Project

The book Mind, Machine, and Will by Professors Daniel Plate and James Hutson presents a radical and challenging framework for understanding creativity in the age of AI. It argues that intention, agency, and the "soul" of the artist are philosophical illusions, and that the value of art lies only in its public reception and function.

This blog is an experiment. It's a collection of imaginary, "what-if" essays from the perspectives of various YouTubers, bloggers, authors, and thinkers. Each post is a fictional response, an attempt to stress-test the provocative ideas in the Plate and Hutson book from a different point of view. How would our digital creators and artists grapple with a world that devalues the human ghost in the machine?

These posts are not real, but they aim to explore very real anxieties about the future of art and humanity.
